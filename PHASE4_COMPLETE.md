### 3. **Professional Scene Breakdown** 🎞️
Each scene includes EVERYTHING a production team needs:

#### Actor & Performance
- Actor description (age, appearance, type)
- Emotion and character state
- Exact dialogue word-for-word
- Detailed physical actions

#### Camera Setup
- **Shot Type**: Wide, Medium, Close-up, ECU, Two-shot, OTS
- **Angle**: Eye level, High angle, Low angle, Dutch angle
- **Movement**: Static, Pan, Dolly, Handheld, Gimbal follow, Crane
- **Lens**: Specific focal lengths (35mm, 50mm, 85mm, zoom range)
- **Framing**: Rule of thirds, Centered, Headroom notes

#### Lighting Design (3-Point Setup)
- **Setup Type**: 3-point, Natural window, Dramatic single source
- **Mood**: Soft/warm, Harsh/clinical, Moody, etc.
- **Key Light**: Position, angle, quality (e.g., "Softbox camera right, 45°")
- **Fill Light**: Intensity and position
- **Back Light**: Rim light for separation

#### Audio Design
- **Dialogue Recording**: Mic types and placement
- **Music**: Genre and mood (Upbeat electronic, Emotional piano)
- **Sound Effects**: Specific SFX list per scene
- **Ambience**: Background environmental sounds

#### Visual Elements
- **Screen Text**: Text overlays to display
- **B-Roll**: Supplementary footage needed
- **Props**: All physical items required
- **Wardrobe**: Clothing style and colors
- **Color Grade**: Post-production look (Warm, Cool, Cinematic teal-orange)

#### Technical Details
- **Location**: Specific setting
- **Time of Day**: Morning, Golden hour, Night, etc.
- **Duration**: Scene length in seconds
- **Transition**: How to move to next scene
- **Director Notes**: Special instructions

   - Equipment, crew, locations, budget, days

   - Color palette, mood board, references, notes
8. ✅ **Post-Production Guide**
   - Editing, music, transitions, pacing
